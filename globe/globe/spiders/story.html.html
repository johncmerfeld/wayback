Jeff Sessions to speak in Boston Thursday

By Christina Prignano Globe staff  September 20, 2017

Attorney General Jeff Sessions will be in Boston on Thursday.

Sessions is scheduled to speak to federal law enforcement officials at the US Attorney’s Office at the Moakley federal courthouse in South Boston, according to a press release from the Department of Justice.

Advertisement

The afternoon remarks will focus on “transnational criminal organizations,” according to the release.

Sessions’ visit to the city comes as the attorney general is under fire over the Trump administration’s decision to end DACA, an Obama program that offers young immigrants brought into the United States as children work permits and protection from deportation.

Sessions was the one to announce the end of the program earlier this month, telling reporters that there is “nothing compassionate about the failure to enforce immigration laws.”

The decision has sparked a number of protests, including one in Boston last Saturday afternoon that drew hundreds and was sponsored by more than a dozen organizations.

Meanwhile, a Facebook event posted by ACLU Massachusetts promises to greet Sessions at the Boston courthouse Thursday with an “unwelcoming party.”
